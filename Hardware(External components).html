Input component:

  1. The keyboard: enters texts using numbers, alphabets, or even special symbols.

  2. The mouse: It is used to mark a specific part of a computer screen, or to choose any of the different menu commands, and to maximize and minimize windows.

  3. The Scanner: The scanner allows the user to enter data directly from a paper document into a computer, by taking a picture of this.

  4. The Touch Screen: It is a screen that is interacted with by the user through the sense of touch, such as those on the bank teller.

  5. The Digital camera: A large number of pictures can be taken using the digital camera, then transferred and entered into the computer by connecting the camera to the device.

  6. The Microphone: is a device that captures audio by converting sound waves into an electrical signal. This signal can be amplified as an analog signal or may be converted to a digital signal, which can be processed by a computer or other digital audio device.

  7. barcode reader: is an optical scanner that can read printed barcodes, decode the data contained in the barcode and send the data to a computer. Like a flatbed scanner, it consists of a light source, a lens and a light sensor translating for optical impulses into electrical signals. Additionally, nearly all barcode readers contain decoder circuitry that can analyze the barcode's image data provided by the sensor and sending the barcode's content to the scanner's output port.

output component:

  1. The Monitor: It is the main output device in a computer.

  2. The Speakers: It is the audio output device via a computer.

  3. printer: is a device that accepts text and graphic output from a computer and transfers the information to paper, usually to standard size sheets of paper. Printers vary in size, speed, sophistication, and cost. In general, more expensive printers are used for higher-resolution color printing.

  4. The plotter: is a computer hardware device much like a printer that is used for printing vector graphics. Instead of toner, plotters use a pen, pencil, marker, or another writing tool to draw multiple, continuous lines onto paper rather than a series of dots like a traditional printer.

  5. The projector: is an output device that projects an image onto a large surface, such as a white screen or wall. It may be used an alternative to a monitor or television when showing video or images to a large group of people.

  6. The headset: is a hardware device that connects to a telephone or computer that allow the user to talk and listen while keeping their hands free.
